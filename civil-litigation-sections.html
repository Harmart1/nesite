The Litigation Process

Understanding the steps involved in civil litigation can help you prepare for what lies ahead.

1

Initial Consultation

We meet to discuss your situation, evaluate your case's merits, and outline potential strategies and outcomes.

2

Pre-Litigation Assessment

We gather and analyze evidence, research applicable law, and determine the strongest approach for your case.

3

Demand & Negotiation

When appropriate, we initiate contact with opposing parties to explore settlement possibilities before formal litigation.

4

Pleadings

If litigation proceeds, we prepare and file necessary court documents to initiate or respond to legal action.

5

Discovery

We exchange information with opposing parties through document productions, written questions, and examinations under oath.

6

Pre-Trial Procedures

We participate in case management conferences, motions, and mandatory mediation attempts as required by the court.

7

Trial

If necessary, we present your case in court with thorough preparation, compelling evidence, and persuasive advocacy.

8

Enforcement or Appeal

Following judgment, we pursue enforcement of favorable decisions or evaluate appeal options if needed.

Frequently Asked Questions

The timeline for civil litigation varies significantly depending on the complexity of the case, the court's schedule, and whether settlement is possible. Simple disputes might resolve in a few months, while complex cases can take one to three years to reach trial. During your consultation, we'll provide a more specific estimate based on your circumstances.

Litigation costs include legal fees, court filing fees, expenses for gathering evidence, expert witnesses if needed, and potentially costs awards if unsuccessful. We offer different fee arrangements including hourly rates, flat fees for certain services, and in some cases, contingency arrangements. We provide transparent cost estimates at the outset and keep you informed of any changes throughout your case.

The majority of civil cases (over 90%) settle before reaching trial. We approach each case with settlement in mind, while preparing thoroughly as if trial will occur. This dual approach ensures we can negotiate from a position of strength while being ready to proceed to court if necessary. The decision to settle is always yours, and we'll provide our professional recommendation based on the specific circumstances of your case.

To make the most of your initial consultation, please bring any documents relevant to your case, such as contracts, correspondence, photographs, reports, or other records related to the dispute. Also prepare a chronological summary of key events if possible. This helps us understand your situation quickly and provide more specific guidance during our first meeting.

Your level of involvement will vary depending on the nature of your case. Generally, you'll need to provide information, review and approve documents, participate in discovery processes, and make key decisions about settlement offers. We handle the legal work and court appearances, but your input remains essential throughout. We'll set clear expectations about your participation requirements at the beginning of our engagement.
